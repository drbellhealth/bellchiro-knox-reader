Adjustment & Manual Therapy

Chiropractic adjustment — also called spinal manipulation — is the central technique. The chiropractor uses a precise, controlled force to a specific joint of the spine or extremity to restore motion. Most adjustments are quick and low-discomfort; the audible 'pop' some people associate with the adjustment is just gas releasing from the joint capsule, not a bone breaking or moving out of place. A single visit can include adjustments to several segments depending on what the exam shows.

Spinal Decompression & Traction

Non-surgical spinal decompression uses a motorized table to gently stretch the spine in a controlled cycle. The goal is to reduce pressure on intervertebral discs and nerve roots — useful for patients with sciatica, herniated or bulging discs, or chronic lower back pain that hasn't responded to manual care alone. A typical decompression program runs over several weeks of short sessions.

Soft-Tissue & Rehabilitation

Most musculoskeletal pain involves the muscles and fascia around the joint, not just the joint itself. Soft-tissue work — myofascial release, trigger-point therapy, instrument-assisted soft-tissue mobilization — is commonly used alongside adjustments to address tight or guarded muscles that would otherwise pull the joint right back out of alignment.

Wellness & Maintenance Care

After the initial complaint resolves, many patients move to a less-frequent maintenance schedule — every few weeks or once a month — to keep symptoms from returning. This is optional and a personal call. The honest answer most chiropractors will give is that maintenance care is reasonable for some patients (heavy desk workers, athletes, people with prior major injuries) and unnecessary for others.

What a First Visit Looks Like

A first visit typically starts with a written history, a focused exam (range of motion, orthopedic tests, palpation), and a discussion of what the chiropractor found and what care would look like. Imaging is ordered only if the exam suggests it's needed — most cases of common back pain do not require an X-ray or MRI to start care. Plan on 45 minutes to an hour for the first appointment; follow-up visits are usually shorter.
